**Riddle:**

You are a lone astronaut stranded on a distant planet.  You have three containers: one blue, one red, and one green. Each container holds a different type of liquid:

* **Blue:**  The color of the planet's sky.
* **Red:** The hottest fire on Earth.
* **Green:** The color of a thriving forest.

But there is a twist: the liquids inside are not their usual states.  
  
**The twist:**

* Each liquid inside the container has been supercharged to a specific state of matter based on its color.  
    * Blue: Liquid nitrogen,  solid
    * Red: Liquid mercury, solid
    * Green: Liquid oxygen, gas

To survive, you need to figure out which container holds the liquid needed to create a specific form of energy. 

**The Goal:**

The form of energy you need depends on the "energy level" on the planet's surface.   It is a question of logic and deduction.

**Your task:**

Determine the container that holds the liquid for a specific energy level based on the planet's surface. 

**Reasoning:**

This puzzle relies on the understanding of the different states of matter and how they relate to energy. 

* **Liquid nitrogen:** Can solidify and become a very cold gas under pressure, allowing for efficient heat transfer for various applications, such as rocket fuel. 
* **Liquid mercury:** Has high density, making it useful in thermometers and for creating dense, conductive alloys for other applications. 
* **Liquid oxygen:** Highly reactive, it can be used to create energy through rapid chemical reactions, which could be used for propulsion, for example.
