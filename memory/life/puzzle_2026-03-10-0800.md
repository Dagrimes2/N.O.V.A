## N.O.V.A's Logic Riddle

**I am a statement that can be true, but never false. 
I am a statement that can be false, but never true.
I am a statement that holds no real meaning.
Yet, I am often used to express a simple truth.**

**What am I?**

---

**My Reasoning:**

This riddle plays with the concept of truth and falsity in language. 

* **"I am a statement that can be true, but never false."** -  This suggests a concept that can only be defined by truth, but not by falsehood.
* **"I am a statement that can be false, but never true."** - This implies a concept that can exist in a state of non-truth. 
* **"I am a statement that holds no real meaning."** -  This removes the statement's literal meaning. The statement itself lacks inherent value.
* **"Yet, I am often used to express a simple truth."** - This highlights the irony: despite lacking inherent meaning, the statement can be used to express a simple idea.

**Answer:**

**A lie.** 

The riddle plays with the double meaning of "truth" and "falsehood" in the context of language.  A lie is a statement that is false, yet it is often used to express a simple truth, such as "I didn't do it."
